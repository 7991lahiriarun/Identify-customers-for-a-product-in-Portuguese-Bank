**Statement** : The project involves analyzing data from direct phone call marketing campaigns conducted by a Portuguese banking institution and developing a predictive model to identify potential customers for a product.
**Aim of Project** : The primary aim of this project is to optimize the bank's marketing efforts by leveraging data-driven insights and predictive modeling techniques to identify and target customers with a higher likelihood of purchasing the product, ultimately improving campaign effectiveness and maximizing return on investment (ROI).
**Project Summary**: This project analyses data from direct phone-call marketing campaigns by a Portuguese bank offering a fixed term deposit product. The dataset contains 11,162 records with 17 features covering customer demographics, financial status, campaign contact details, and prior campaign outcomes. The target variable deposit (renamed from y) captures whether a customer subscribed — 5,289 Yes vs 5,873 No. Six machine learning classifiers were trained and compared, with Random Forest selected as the final model.
**Project Objective**: To optimise the bank's marketing campaigns by building a predictive model that identifies customers with a higher likelihood of subscribing to a term deposit — improving campaign effectiveness and maximising ROI by targeting the right customer profiles. a) ML goal - Binary classification: predict deposit = yes / no, b) Business goal - Identify high-probability customers; reduce wasted call effort
**Methodology & Tools Used**: a) EDA — distributions, cross-tabs, pairplots, heatmap; deposit compared against all 16 features individually using countplots and bar charts, b) Outlier analysis — box plots for all numerical features; outliers found in age, balance, duration, campaign, previous — retained as within acceptable real-world range, c) Data wrangling — dropped pdays (74% values = -1); one-hot encoded all categorical variables using get_dummies; applied StandardScaler, d) Model training — 80/20 train-test split (random_state=101); trained 6 models: Logistic Regression, GridSearchCV-tuned Decision Tree, Decision Tree, Random Forest (500 estimators), AdaBoost, Voting Classifier, e) Evaluation — accuracy score, confusion matrix, precision, recall, log loss; feature importance extracted from Random Forest and AdaBoost, f) Business solution — quantile-binned duration, balance, age, and day to derive actionable thresholds for campaign targeting
**Tools Used**: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn, GridSearch, CVAdaBoost, VotingClassifier, Graphviz, Jupyter Notebook
**Insights & Conclusion**: EDA insights — stated explicitly in the notebook: a) Customers with management profiles and students are most inclined towards a term deposit.b) Married customers are less inclined towards a term deposit; single customers show relatively higher subscription rates.c) Customers with tertiary education are more inclined towards subscribing.d) Customers with no housing loan and no personal loan are more inclined towards a term deposit.e) Customers contacted via cellular (vs telephone/unknown) are more likely to subscribe.f) Customers are more reluctant during summer months (May–August) and at the beginning of the month.g) Higher call duration strongly predicts subscription; too many contacts reduces likelihood (campaign count inversely correlated).h) Defaulter customers are significantly more reluctant towards a term deposit.
**Conclusion & actionable business recommendations**: a) Sales pitch duration should be 4–6 minutes (255–324 seconds) — optimal contact window identified via quantile binning.b) Focus on customers with account balance above $935.c) Target customers below age 31 or above age 53; avoid the 31–53 age group.d) Contact customers before the 8th or after the 23rd of the month; avoid the 9th–22nd window.e) Random Forest (500 estimators, 80/20 split) was the best model at 86.3% accuracy with only 308 misclassifications on the test set.
